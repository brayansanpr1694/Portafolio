My Teaching Experience: Programming & Robotics with micro:bit

Published June 5, 2024 Â· By Brayan Santamaria

Last summer, I had the incredible opportunity to teach programming to teenagers using micro:bit devices. This experience taught me as much as it taught my students about coding, robotics, and effective teaching methods.

Why micro:bit?

I chose micro:bit because it's the perfect bridge between abstract programming concepts and tangible results. The immediate physical feedback kept students engaged in ways that pure software couldn't.

Curriculum Design

I structured the course to progress from basic concepts to complex projects:

  • Week 1: Introduction to block programming
  • Week 2: Transition to JavaScript/Python
  • Week 3: Sensor integration and data collection
  • Week 4: Robotics fundamentals
  • Week 5: Final project development
"The moment when a student's robot first responds to their code is pure magic. You can see the exact instant programming becomes real for them."

Challenges Faced

Teaching presented unexpected difficulties:

1. Different Learning Paces

Some students grasped concepts immediately while others needed more time. I implemented a buddy system where advanced students could help others.

2. Hardware Issues

Broken sensors and connectivity problems were frequent. I learned to always have spare parts and multiple troubleshooting approaches ready.

Breakthrough Moments

The most rewarding parts included:

  • Seeing students help each other debug code
  • Watching their excitement when their robots completed obstacle courses
  • Hearing students explain concepts to their parents during demo day

Teaching Techniques That Worked

These approaches proved most effective:

Project-Based Learning

Students learned fastest when working toward concrete goals like building a weather station or line-following robot.

Minimal Lectures

I kept explanations under 10 minutes, followed by hands-on activities. The "show, don't tell" method worked wonders.

Celebrating Failure

We normalized bugs and errors as part of the process, with "best fail of the day" awards that students surprisingly loved.

Student Projects That Impressed Me

Some standout creations included:

  • A smart plant watering system with moisture sensors
  • A digital pet that responded to light and sound
  • A security alarm that sent notifications to parents' phones

What I Learned as a Teacher

This experience taught me:

  • Patience is more valuable than technical knowledge
  • Every student has a unique way of understanding concepts
  • Preparation is crucial, but flexibility is more important
  • Teaching solidifies your own understanding

Resources I Used

  • Official micro:bit Teach Portal
  • Microsoft MakeCode for micro:bit
  • MicroPython for micro:bit
  • micro:bit MicroPython GitHub

Final Thoughts

Teaching programming through robotics was one of the most rewarding experiences of my career. Watching students transform from hesitant beginners to confident creators reminded me why I love technology. I'm already planning next summer's curriculum with more advanced concepts and projects!

Have you taught programming to beginners? What methods worked best for you? Share your experiences in the comments!
